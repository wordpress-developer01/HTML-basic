Lagman

Description

Lagman is a hearty and flavorful noodle dish popular in Central Asia, particularly in countries like Uzbekistan, Kazakhstan, and Kyrgyzstan. It features hand-pulled noodles served with a savory stir-fried topping of meat and vegetables in a rich, spiced broth.

This dish is beloved for its perfect combination of chewy noodles, tender meat, and aromatic spices, making it a comforting and satisfying meal for any occasion.

Steps to Cook

  1. Prepare the noodles: If making homemade noodles, knead the dough, let it rest, and pull it into thin strands. If using store-bought noodles, boil them according to package instructions.
  2. Heat oil in a large pan or wok. Add diced lamb or beef and cook until browned.
  3. Add chopped onions, garlic, and bell peppers. Stir-fry until the vegetables begin to soften.
  4. Incorporate diced tomatoes, carrots, and potatoes. Cook for a few minutes before adding water or broth to create a sauce.
  5. Season with salt, pepper, paprika, cumin, and optionally chili flakes for a bit of heat. Simmer until the vegetables are tender.
  6. Serve the cooked noodles in bowls and ladle the meat and vegetable sauce on top. Garnish with fresh herbs like cilantro or parsley.
